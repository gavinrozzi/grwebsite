Presented a comprehensive overview of OPRAmachine, New Jersey's first statewide freedom of information platform, to journalism students and faculty at Montclair State University. The webinar covered:

- The fundamentals of New Jersey's Open Public Records Act (OPRA)
- How technology can reduce barriers to government transparency
- Best practices for filing effective public records requests
- Case studies of impactful stories uncovered through OPRA
- The role of civic technology in strengthening democracy

The presentation included a live demonstration of the OPRAmachine platform, showing how users can file requests, track their status, and access previously released records. Attendees included journalism students, faculty members, and local reporters interested in investigative techniques.

**Key Takeaways:**
- Over 75,000 public records requests processed through OPRAmachine
- Platform saves requesters an average of 40% time on follow-ups
- Open access to released records prevents duplicate requests
- Technology enables transparency at scale

This talk is part of ongoing efforts to educate journalists, students, and citizens about their rights to access government information and how modern tools can make that access more efficient and effective.
