Appeared on the Civic Tech Voices podcast to discuss the intersection of technology, transparency, and civic engagement. The wide-ranging conversation covered technical architecture, user experience design, and policy considerations for building government-facing platforms.

## Episode Topics

### Technical Architecture
- Building scalable platforms for government use
- Choosing the right technology stack (Ruby on Rails)
- Database design for public records management
- Security and privacy considerations

### User Experience
- Designing for multiple user types (citizens, journalists, researchers)
- Accessibility requirements for government platforms
- Mobile-first design principles
- Reducing friction in government interactions

### Policy & Impact
- Working with government agencies on transparency initiatives
- Legal framework of open records laws
- Measuring platform success and impact
- Future of digital government services

### Lessons Learned
- Start with user needs, not technology
- Government adoption takes time and relationship building
- Documentation and education are as important as the platform
- Open source creates trust and enables replication
